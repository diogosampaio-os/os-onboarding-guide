Common First-Week Questions

What is Agentic Systems Engineering and how does it shape Digital's work?
ASE is OutSystems' new platform category, announced March 31, 2026 — repositioning from low-code to the platform that governs, orchestrates, and scales enterprise AI agents. For the Digital team, this means your internal tools and Big Bet experiments are part of the same transformation the company is selling externally. You're proving the concept from the inside. Read the ASE FAQ and watch the product sizzle demo to understand the strategic context.
What is the Big Bet I'm assigned to and where do I start?
Ask Fábio for a Big Bet brief walkthrough on Day 1. Before the meeting, find the brief in Google Drive and read it top to bottom — problem statement, desired outcomes, and Scout mission statement. Then check the WIP experiment doc to understand what's already been tried. Your first job is to understand the problem space well enough to run a useful experiment in week 2, not to start building.
What does Digital onboarding look like and what are the milestones?
Digital onboarding is run directly by Fábio — there is no formal programme separate from the work itself. Typical milestones: dev environment running by end of Day 1, first experiment or PR by end of week 1, first evidence doc shared with stakeholders by end of week 2, and a Scout recommendation (kill/continue/convert) by end of week 3. Ask Fábio how these apply to your specific Big Bet.
How does the GenAI Suite relate to my work?
The GenAI Suite is OutSystems' internal tooling framework that Fábio's team actively supports via #digital-genai-team-dev-support. Your Big Bet experiments will likely build on or integrate with the GenAI Suite — understanding its capabilities on ODC and O11 is important context. Read the support channel history and ask Fábio for the integration patterns most relevant to your Scout.
How is performance measured on the Digital team?
Performance is evaluated on the quality and velocity of evidence produced in Scout missions, and on the impact of shipped internal tools. Clear experiment design, honest documentation of what worked and what didn't, and recommendations the business can act on. For ongoing apps like V2MOM, it's delivery quality and reliability. Ask Fábio how the review cycle works for your role.
When should I escalate a blocker vs. solve it myself?
Escalate to Fábio when: you're blocked for more than a day on something outside your control, a stakeholder is unresponsive and it's delaying the Scout timeline, or an experiment result suggests the Big Bet direction needs to change. For GenAI Suite / API questions post in #digital-genai-team-dev-support. For infrastructure or access issues, post in #digital-spot. Scout timelines are short — surface blockers early.
Where do I find existing work, documentation, and experiment history?
Three places: (1) Google Drive — all Big Bet briefs, Scout WIP docs, experiment notes, and POC slides. Search for your Big Bet name. (2) Confluence — engineering standards, ADRs, and team process docs. (3) Jira — all current and historical tickets for your team's work. Before starting any experiment, verify it hasn't already been tried — the WIP doc is the authoritative source of what's been tested.
What's the most important thing to get right in the first 30 days?
Understand the problem before building the solution. The Digital team's biggest failure mode is shipping something elegant that doesn't match what end users actually need. In your first 30 days: read every existing document about your Big Bet, interview at least 3 revenue team end users, and run experiments with clear pass/fail criteria before writing production code. The engineers who make the biggest impact combine technical skill with deep user empathy.

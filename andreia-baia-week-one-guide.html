Common First-Week Questions

What is Agentic Systems Engineering and why does it matter for Finance?
ASE is OutSystems' new platform category announced March 31, 2026 — repositioning from low-code to the platform that governs, orchestrates, and scales enterprise AI. For Finance, this is the strategic bet you're helping to fund and measure. Understanding the company's product direction helps you build a better budget, ask better questions in variance reviews, and be a more credible business partner to the teams building it. Watch the product sizzle demo and read the ASE FAQ in your first week.
What is the close process and what will I own?
Ask José Chaveiro for a close calendar walkthrough on Day 1 and a copy of the prior period close pack. The close is Finance's primary recurring deliverable — every month the team runs a structured sequence of sub-ledger closes, journal entries, reconciliations, and reporting. Your ownership within that cycle depends on your role and function. Before your first close, make sure you know: which journals you're posting, which accounts you're reconciling, and who approves your work.
What does Finance onboarding look like and what are the milestones?
Finance onboarding at OutSystems is structured around the close cycle. Typical milestones: system access live by end of Day 1, close process shadowed by end of week 1, first independent journal entries or reconciliations by end of week 2, and full ownership of your assigned close responsibilities by month 2. Ask José Chaveiro for the full onboarding checklist and your specific timeline.
How is Finance performance measured?
Finance performance is measured on close quality and speed (hitting the D+5 target with clean reconciliations), forecast accuracy (actual vs. forecast variance), audit readiness (no material findings), and business partnership effectiveness. Ask José Chaveiro for your specific KPIs and how they're tracked.
When should I escalate a finance issue vs. resolve it myself?
Escalate to José Chaveiro immediately when: a reconciling item is material and unexplained, a journal entry is being disputed, a system error may have affected reported numbers, or a close deadline is at risk. Finance errors compound — a posting mistake caught on Day 2 is much easier to fix than one caught after the reporting pack is delivered. When in doubt, ask before you post. Post in #digital-spot for system access issues.
Where do I find Finance policies, templates, and working papers?
Three places: (1) Confluence / SharePoint — Finance policies and procedures, delegation of authority, close checklist templates, and audit documentation. (2) Google Drive Finance shared folder — prior period close packs, reconciliation working papers, and budget models. (3) #dep-finance — ask your team for links to live working files. Never start a reconciliation or journal entry from scratch — find the existing template first.
What is the ONE Conference and how does Finance participate?
ONE '26 is OutSystems' annual flagship customer conference, June 2–3, 2026. Finance typically attends in a support role — observing customer sessions, supporting executive participation, and understanding the commercial momentum the company is building. The application deadline was April 24 — ask José Chaveiro whether Finance has specific participation goals or if late registration is still possible.
What's the most important thing to get right in the first 30 days?
Own your first close cleanly. In Finance, credibility is built through the accuracy and reliability of your numbers — not through big ideas. In your first 30 days: understand every account and journal entry you're responsible for, reconcile every item you own before sign-off, and never let a deadline slip without flagging it in advance. Accuracy first, then speed, then insight.
